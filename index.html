Importance of Financial Stability

Everybody knows that keeping your finances in check is important, but knowing the importance and understanding how to actually stay financially stable are two very different things. Without financial stability, you could end up jobless, homeless, and hopeless. Even people like Nick Cage have trouble handling their money! Don't end up blowing through $150 million, going broke, then owing the IRS $13 million! Find out how to properly manage your finances below!

Being Organized

  • Tracking Expenses

    When staying orgainzed, keeping track of where the money goes is extremely important. One way to accomplish this is by holding on to a record of your purchases whether they be physical or digital. Update a balance book or excel sheet whenever you spend with a credit card to make sure you don't accidentally go into debt. Staying on track of expenses helps protect you from overspending, fraudulent spending, poor purchasing decisions, and many more.

  • Utilizing Net Income

    Being financially organized helps determine whether your life choices are giving you a profit as well as an idea of how much profit. Understanding how much one makes through their job doesn't mean anything without a firm grasp over how much is left after subracting expenses, taxes, food and other routine costs. The net income is what dictates how much money can be spent on luxuries, investments and other non-essential costs that do not involve survival as well as how extravagant someone can be.

  • Keeping Emergency Funds

    Although organization can deal with most predictable cases, preparing for the unplanned is equally essential. In the case of car accidents, natural disasters, hospital visits, and other surprise events that occur during peoples' lives, emergency funds are necessary to deal with the financial cost. Simply having some extra unused/uninvested funds covers you in the event of an unexpected financial blow, and can help prevent you from going into debt and provide some peace of mind.

Investing Tips

After becoming organized, the question stands "What do I do with my extra unassigned money?" To that, the answer is to invest in stocks. This might sound scary to someone without any knowledge in stock trading, but anyone and everyone can get into stocks, especially with the current number of investing apps and companies followed by numerous sources of investment advice readily available in the internet today. Stock trading isn't as hard or dangerous as it once was decades ago and it sure beats leaving that hard earned money to collect dust in a bank account. When investing, there are only a few simple tips to follow: do research on how current events affect the market, buy with the mentality that you are becoming a part owner of that business, plan ahead and come up with reasons why a company should or shouldn't be bought or sold, and be calm and patient with investments. Research allows for the greatest opportunity for net profit compared to buying randomly. When treating yourself as part owner of a business, it holds accountability towards yourself as an investor and pushes yourself to understand the company at a deeper level. Creating reasons for particular investments removes the idea of impulse buying and selling giving your decisions a much less margin of error. Staying calm and patient also helps avoid making decisions based on emotion as there are times when the price fluxuates and while the sight of a random large drop might strike fear, it is normally followed with its share of large rises. Investing might not seem like an important or essential part of being financially stable, but it's benefits are subtle and life-changing.

Available Resources

  • Mint Intuit

    Mint is an online service that allows users to track bank, credit card, investment, and loan balances and transactions through a single user interface, as well as create budgets and set financial goals. It is currently owned and run by the same popular financial-software company Intuit - the same company behind TurboTax.

  • Robinhood

    Robinhood is a company that offers a mobile app and website that allows people to invest in stocks, ETFs, and options as well as facilitates crypto trading. This app allows users to skip the middle man and buy and sell stocks directly from the stock market without any hinderences.

  • Charles Schwab

    Charles Schwab is a multinational financial services company that offers a wide range of investment advice, products & services, including brokerage & retirement accounts, ETFs, online trading & more. Charles Schwab is a large trusted company that will provide a starting place for when and what to invest in.

  • Certified Public Acountant

    Certified Public Acountants (CPA) are another resource in keeping track of financials as well as places for investment advice. Acountants spend years studying the field of finance for the sole reason of helping others who are having trouble understanding and handling their own money.
